\section{Project Overview}

This project builds a supervised binary classifier to distinguish spam tokens (label\,=\,0)
from legitimate tokens (label\,=\,1) on the Ethereum blockchain.
Data covers \textbf{1,000 consecutive blocks} ($\approx$\,20 million token transfer events)
starting at block 20{,}000{,}000.

Labels are defined as follows:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Legit (1):} Contract address appears in the verified token list (\texttt{token\_labels.csv}).
  \item \textbf{Spam (0):} Token shares the same symbol as a verified token but uses a different contract address (symbol collision).
  \item \textbf{Unlabeled (NaN):} Unverified tokens without a known collision --- excluded from supervised training.
\end{itemize}

All features are aggregated at the \textbf{token (contract address) level} from the raw transfer-event data.
The final labeled dataset contains \textbf{3,606 tokens} (spam\,=\,2,007\,|\,legit\,=\,1,599).

\section{Raw Transfer Data Columns}

The primary data file is \texttt{transfers\_20000000.parquet}.
Below are the raw columns used as inputs for feature engineering.

\vspace{0.5em}
\begin{tabular}{>{\ttfamily}p{4.2cm} p{10cm}}
\toprule
\normalfont\textbf{Column} & \textbf{Description} \\
\midrule
blockNum          & Block number (hexadecimal string) in which the transfer was included \\
hash              & Transaction hash uniquely identifying the on-chain transaction \\
from              & Sender Ethereum address (20-byte hex) \\
to                & Recipient Ethereum address (20-byte hex) \\
value             & Transfer amount in decimal token units (float; null for ERC-721/1155) \\
asset             & Token symbol as reported by the Alchemy API (e.g., USDT, PEPE) \\
category          & Transfer category: \texttt{erc20}, \texttt{token}, \texttt{erc721}, \texttt{erc1155}, \texttt{specialnft} \\
rawContract.address & Contract address of the token (lowercase hex) \\
rawContract.decimal & Token decimal precision (e.g., 18 for most ERC-20 tokens) \\
rawContract.value  & Raw transfer amount as a hex string before decimal conversion \\
metadata.blockTimestamp & ISO timestamp of the block \\
tokenId           & NFT token ID (only for ERC-721 / ERC-1155; null for ERC-20) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\vspace{0.5em}
\textit{Note: only transfers with category in \{erc20, token, erc721, erc1155, specialnft\}
are used ($\approx$\,2.16 million rows after filtering).}

\section{Engineered Features}

All 27 features below are computed per unique \texttt{contract\_addr}
(i.e., one row per token) and used as the model input matrix $X$.

\vspace{0.5em}
\noindent\begin{tabular}{>{\ttfamily}p{4.6cm} >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{4.0cm} >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{5.6cm}}
\toprule
\normalfont\textbf{Feature} & \textbf{Calculation} & \textbf{Spam signal / interpretation} \\
\midrule

\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{Activity \& volume}} \\[2pt]

n\_transfers
  & \texttt{COUNT(hash)} per token
  & Spam tokens typically have a low total number of transfers (one-off airdrop) \\[4pt]

n\_unique\_senders
  & \texttt{NUNIQUE(from)} per token
  & Spam is sent by very few addresses (often just 1 bot); highest correlation with label ($r{=}0.61$) \\[4pt]

n\_unique\_receivers
  & \texttt{NUNIQUE(to)} per token
  & Spam airdrops hit many receivers; combined with senders gives the airdrop ratio \\[6pt]

\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{Transfer value}} \\[2pt]

value\_mean
  & \texttt{MEAN(value)} per token
  & Less discriminative alone; useful in combination with std \\[4pt]

value\_std
  & \texttt{STD(value)} per token
  & Spam uses uniform fixed amounts (std $\approx$ 0); legit tokens have varied transfer sizes \\[4pt]

value\_null\_ratio
  & \texttt{MEAN(value IS NULL)} per token
  & Spam tokens (especially fake ERC-20s) often lack decimal metadata, producing null values \\[4pt]

zero\_value\_ratio
  & \texttt{MEAN(value == 0)} per token
  & Spam airdrops frequently send zero-value transfers to claim wallet attention \\[4pt]

unique\_values\_count
  & \texttt{NUNIQUE(value)} per token
  & Spam uses very few distinct amounts (often exactly 1); legit tokens show diverse amounts \\[6pt]

\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{Block distribution}} \\[2pt]

n\_distinct\_blocks
  & \texttt{NUNIQUE(block\_int)} per token
  & Spam is deployed in a burst of 1--2 blocks; $\approx$78\% of spam in a single block vs 12\% for legit \\[4pt]

block\_range
  & $\texttt{MAX(block\_int)} - \texttt{MIN(block\_int)}$
  & Complementary to \texttt{n\_distinct\_blocks}; 0 means all transfers happened in one block \\[6pt]

\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{Ratio \& derived}} \\[2pt]

sender\_receiver\_ratio
  & $\mathit{senders} \;/\; \mathit{receivers}$
  & Core airdrop signal: few senders $\to$ many receivers gives ratio $\ll 1$; spam median $\approx 0.001$ \\[4pt]

transfers\_per\_block
  & $\mathit{n\_transfers} \;/\; \mathit{n\_blocks}$
  & High value indicates a burst (many transfers in one block), characteristic of automated airdrops \\[6pt]

\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{Concentration}} \\[2pt]

top1\_sender\_share
  & $\max(\text{per-sender count}) \;/\; n\_transfers$
  & Close to 1.0 if a single bot drove all transfers; strong indicator of automated spam \\[4pt]

receiver\_concentration
  & Gini coeff.\ of transfers-per-receiver
  & High Gini ($\to 1$) means transfers are concentrated on few receivers; low means evenly spread \\[6pt]

\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{External / categorical}} \\[2pt]

category\_entropy
  & Shannon entropy of the transfer category distribution
  & Computed as $-\sum p_i \log p_i$ over category proportions; low entropy = homogeneous transfers \\[4pt]

sender\_is\_labeled
  & Binary: 1 if any sender address appears in \texttt{account\_labels.csv}
  & Legit tokens are more likely sent by known DEX routers or protocol contracts \\[6pt]

\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{Graph-based (from transfer graph via NetworkX)}} \\[2pt]

in\_degree\_mean
  & Mean in-degree of receivers in the token's directed transfer graph
  & Low $\to$ airdrop to fresh wallets with no prior activity \\[4pt]

out\_degree\_max
  & Max out-degree of any sender in the token's directed transfer graph
  & High $\to$ single bot spraying transfers to many addresses \\[4pt]

star\_topology\_ratio
  & Fraction of senders with out-degree $\ge 10 \times$ in-degree
  & High $\to$ 1 sender $\to$ N receivers star topology \\[4pt]

n\_connected\_components
  & Number of weakly connected components in the token's transfer graph
  & High $\to$ fragmented, synthetic graph \\[6pt]

\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{Temporal (from block numbers)}} \\[2pt]

activity\_decay\_rate
  & Transfers in first 10 unique blocks / transfers in last 10 unique blocks
  & High $\to$ front-loaded activity that dies quickly \\[4pt]

inter\_block\_time\_var
  & Variance of block gaps between consecutive transfers
  & Low $\to$ robotic, uniform timing between transfers \\[4pt]

burst\_duration\_ratio
  & $(\text{block\_range}) \;/\; n\_transfers$
  & Low $\to$ dense burst airdrop compressed in few blocks \\[6pt]

\multicolumn{3}{l}{\textit{MEV / Transaction (from \texttt{txs\_20000000} \& \texttt{zeromev\_20000000})}} \\[2pt]

is\_deploy\_mev
  & Binary: 1 if any sender/receiver of the token appears in MEV data
  & 1 $\to$ deployer or participants engaged in MEV \\[4pt]

mev\_sandwich\_count
  & Number of token addresses overlapping with sandwich attack addresses
  & High $\to$ token targeted by MEV sandwich bots \\[4pt]

mev\_frontrun\_count
  & Number of token addresses overlapping with frontrunning addresses
  & High $\to$ suspicious frontrunning extraction \\[4pt]

gas\_price\_at\_deploy
  & Gas price (Gwei) of the token's earliest transaction
  & Anomalously high/low $\to$ bot activity \\

\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\begin{center}
\textit{Table 1: Description of engineered features used in the spam detection model}
\end{center}

\section{Label Construction \& Leakage Note}

Two intermediate columns are computed during preprocessing but \textbf{excluded from the feature matrix}
because they directly encode the label:

\vspace{0.5em}
\begin{tabular}{>{\ttfamily}p{3.8cm} p{10.4cm}}
\toprule
\normalfont\textbf{Column} & \textbf{Why excluded} \\
\midrule
symbol\_collision & Binary flag set to 1 when a token's symbol matches a verified token but the contract address differs. This is the \emph{definition} of the spam label (label\,=\,0), so including it would be direct data leakage. \\[4pt]
is\_verified & Binary flag set to 1 when the contract address appears in \texttt{token\_labels.csv}. This is the \emph{definition} of the legit label (label\,=\,1). \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\section{Preprocessing Steps}

Before model training, the following transformations are applied (see \texttt{preprocessing.ipynb}):

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Imputation:} Missing values filled with the column median (no nulls were found in the labeled set).
  \item \textbf{Log transform:} \texttt{log1p} applied to 18 right-skewed features:
        \texttt{n\_transfers}, \texttt{n\_unique\_senders}, \texttt{n\_unique\_receivers},
        \texttt{value\_mean}, \texttt{value\_std}, \texttt{sender\_receiver\_ratio},
        \texttt{transfers\_per\_block}, \texttt{n\_distinct\_blocks}, \texttt{block\_range},
        \texttt{unique\_values\_count},
        \texttt{in\_degree\_mean}, \texttt{out\_degree\_max}, \texttt{n\_connected\_components},
        \texttt{inter\_block\_time\_var}, \texttt{burst\_duration\_ratio},
        \texttt{mev\_sandwich\_count}, \texttt{mev\_frontrun\_count}, \texttt{gas\_price\_at\_deploy}.
  \item \textbf{Scaling:} \texttt{RobustScaler} (centers on median, scales by IQR) fit on the training set only and applied to validation and test sets --- prevents data leakage through the scaler.
  \item \textbf{Split:} Stratified 70\,/\,15\,/\,15 train\,/\,val\,/\,test split.
        Train\,=\,2,524\,|\,Val\,=\,541\,|\,Test\,=\,541.
\end{enumerate}

\textit{Note: tree-based models (Random Forest, XGBoost, LightGBM) are trained on the \textbf{unscaled} log-transformed splits since they are scale-invariant. Linear models (Logistic Regression, Naive Bayes) use the scaled splits.}
